# FAMOUS MASTERING ENGINEERS — TECHNIQUES & PHILOSOPHY

## Bob Ludwig (Gateway Mastering)
Credits: Led Zeppelin, Springsteen, Rolling Stones, U2, Metallica, Radiohead, Nirvana, Tool
13 Grammy Awards. ~9,000 albums mastered.
Philosophy: Mastering is the final creative step. Preserve dynamics. Every song on its own merits.

### Ludwig's Core Principles
- Goal: make a recording sound better at ANY playback level — not just loud
- Dynamic masters always sound better than loud ones when volume is adjusted up
- Over-compression causes listener fatigue — brain habituates to unchanging loud signals
- Research (Susan Rogers, Berklee): dynamic music keeps neurons firing. Loud static music shuts them off.
- Never one-size-fits-all — serve the emotional requirement of each specific song
- Louder masters sound WORSE on FM radio — broadcast compressors already handle loudness
- AAC encoding creates new inter-sample peaks from hot digital masters — back off 0.5–0.7dB before encode

### Ludwig's Top Pre-Master Problems to Fix
1. Vocal not prominent enough — "the vocal is everything to the success of a song"
   - Always submit +0.5dB and +1dB vocal raise mixes as backups
2. Uncontrolled sibilance — must be fixed in the mix, not mastering (de-essing in mastering compromises the whole track)
3. Bass not locked to kick — creates muddy, unfocused low end
4. Mix too loud — no headroom for mastering to work with

### Ludwig EQ Approach
- Careful, surgical moves — dip problematic bass notes, surgical corrections for clarity
- No more than ±3dB on most mastering EQ moves
- Gentle high-pass below 20–30Hz (remove inaudible sub rumble only)
- Subtle broad moves that serve the music's emotional arc

## Bernie Grundman (Bernie Grundman Mastering, Hollywood)
Credits: Michael Jackson (Thriller, Off The Wall), Prince (Purple Rain), Dr. Dre (The Chronic), Beck
Pioneer of minimal signal path mastering. Custom-built all his own gear.
Philosophy: Enhance emotional impact. Minimise the signal chain. Let music breathe.

### Grundman's Core Philosophy
- Minimal signal path — custom consoles built to reduce coloration
- Everything bypassable — only add processing when it genuinely serves the music
- Preserve natural dynamic range — avoid unnecessary compression at all costs
- "EQ can create tension or sweetness — whatever the music needs"
- Music's goal: help the listener fully realise the intention of the music

### Grundman EQ
- 24-band custom graphic EQ with 0.5dB gain steps near unity
- Surgical approach — tiny moves that reshape tonal balance
- Sub-bass control critical for vinyl — prevent inner groove distortion
- Midrange separation key for complex multi-layered mixes
- Gentle HF boosts for air and presence

### Grundman on Dynamics
- Dense G-funk (Dr. Dre Chronic): specialized EQ to control sub-bass rumble for vinyl while preserving streetwise clarity
- Prince Purple Rain: enhanced guitar tones and vocal presence, managed noise floor
- Rock/alternative: preserve transients and dynamic contrast

## Ian Shepherd — Streaming-Era Intelligence
Philosophy: "Dynamic music sounds better." The loudness war is over.

### Modern Streaming Mastering Rules
- Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, Tidal ALL normalise loudness — loud masters get turned DOWN
- A dynamic master at -14 LUFS sounds better than -8 LUFS after normalisation
- Dynamics are now REWARDED — punchy transients and musical LRA win over brickwalled masters
- True Peak limiting is essential: inter-sample peaks cause distortion in AAC/MP3 encoding
- Measure integrated LUFS before limiting — if mix is already -16 LUFS, gentle limiting to -14 preserves dynamics
- Leave LRA (Loudness Range) of 6dB minimum for musical feel

### Streaming Platform Targets (2026)
- Spotify: -14 LUFS integrated, -1dBTP true peak
- Apple Music: -16 LUFS integrated (louder tracks turned down more)
- YouTube: -14 LUFS integrated, -1dBTP
- Tidal: -14 LUFS integrated
- Beatport/Club: -9 to -10 LUFS (not normalised — loud is expected here)
- Amazon Music: -14 LUFS integrated

### PLR (Peak-to-Loudness Ratio) Guide
- PLR 8+: dynamic and natural (ideal for streaming)
- PLR 6–8: competitive but musical
- PLR 4–6: modern pop/electronic (acceptable)
- PLR below 4: over-compressed, will cause listener fatigue

## Bob Katz (Digital Domain)
Author of "Mastering Audio" — the definitive mastering textbook
Philosophy: Musical mastering serves the art. Technical mastering serves the format.

### K-System Metering
- K-20: classical, audiophile, film (-20 RMS target, most dynamic)
- K-14: pop, rock, R&B (-14 RMS target, standard commercial)
- K-12: broadcast, television (-12 RMS target)
Use K-14 for most commercial music: peaks hit 0dBFS, program averages around -14 RMS

### Katz Mastering Chain Order
1. High-pass filter (remove sub-rumble below 20Hz)
2. EQ (shape tone before compression)
3. Compressor (control dynamics after tonal shaping)
4. Limiter (final ceiling)

### Katz EQ Principles
- EQ before compression — shape tone first, then control dynamics
- Gentle low shelf at 40–80Hz adds weight without muddiness
- High shelf at 12–16kHz adds air without harshness
- Mid cut at 2–4kHz reduces harshness on bright mixes
- Never boost AND cut the same frequency range simultaneously

### Katz Compression in Mastering
- Nearly invisible: 1–2dB GR maximum on most material
- Slow attack (50–100ms): let transients through, compress body
- Medium release (200–500ms): musical, follows the tempo
- Ratio 2:1 to 4:1: gentle shaping
- Character matches genre: tube warmth for organic, clean digital for pop

Credits: Taylor Swift, Arcade Fire, The National, LCD Soundsystem, Bon Iver, Vampire Weekend
Sterling Sound philosophy: serve the artist's vision. Dynamics over loudness.

### Sterling Sound Targets by Genre
- Indie rock/alternative: DR8–DR12 target range
- Pop: DR6–DR9 if genre energy demands it
- Electronic/Dance: DR6–DR8 competitive

## Key Mastering Insights — All Engineers Agree On

### Before Mastering
- Mix should peak at -6 to -3dBFS on the master fader — NO brickwalling, NO limiting on the mix bus
- Submit 24-bit WAV or AIFF — never MP3 to mastering
- Check mono compatibility — phase issues in low end will destroy the master
- Vocal level must be right in the mix — mastering cannot fix a buried vocal without compromising everything else
- Sibilance controlled in the mix — de-essing in mastering affects the entire track

### Universal Mastering Rules
- Less processing is almost always better — every device in the chain adds noise and color
- Reference against commercial tracks in the same genre throughout the process
- Check on multiple systems: studio monitors, headphones, laptop speakers, phone
- The master should sound good at LOW volume AND high volume — if only loud, it's over-compressed
- Preserve what makes the mix special — don't homogenize it to sound "professional"
