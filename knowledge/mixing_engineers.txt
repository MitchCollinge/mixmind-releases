# WORLD-CLASS MIXING ENGINEER TECHNIQUES

## CHRIS LORD-ALGE (CLA) — Rock & Pop Mixing Legend
Credits: Green Day, Foo Fighters, My Chemical Romance, Bruce Springsteen, Muse

### Core Philosophy
- "Excitement trumps perfection" — make mixes exciting, not technically perfect
- Mix at low volumes (like NS-10s) — if it sounds powerful quietly, it'll destroy at volume
- Push all faders up immediately and get a balance — don't solo and tinker first
- EQ everything simultaneously because everything reacts off everything else
- Automate like a live performance — bold moves, 15-30 seconds at a time

### Drums (CLA Method)
- Parallel compression on drums ALWAYS — separate room mic pass with no cymbals for aggressive compression
- Unlink left/right on parallel compressor — asymmetric compression creates natural feel
- On parallel bus: boost 3dB at 8kHz and 3dB at 60Hz to add emphasis
- Master bus chain: SSL Compressor → Limiter → Pultec EQ
- SSL bus compressor: threshold -1.5 to -3dB, ratio 2:1 — if it pumps, pull back faders
- Room mics: compress HARD since cymbals are on separate pass

### Vocals (CLA Method)
- Normalize all vocals to 0dBFS before processing
- 1176 compressor with moderate attack, FAST release — 15-20dB of gain reduction is not unusual
- Don't tweak attack/release obsessively — push it hard and make it exciting
- Use L1/L2 limiter on vocals for "spit and attitude" — works almost like an EQ
- HPF at 70Hz on vocals always
- Boost 8-9kHz aggressively (he's boosted 9dB at 8kHz on SSL EQ)
- Parallel distortion on reverb return (Devil-Loc style) for aggression

### EQ Philosophy (CLA)
- Mostly ADDITIVE EQ — large boosts at lows and highs, minimal mid cuts
- Boost lows and highs broadly: keeps midrange character intact, avoids resonance
- Boosting before compression: high end boost makes compressor act as de-esser
- "Magic frequencies": 5kHz for piano presence, 8kHz for vocal air and spit
- EQ quickly — calibrate ears by quickly sweeping between dark and bright

### Panning (CLA)
- LCR panning ONLY — left, right, or center. No subtle panning
- Vocals, kick, snare, bass → center always
- Guitars → hard left and right (±0.8 to ±1.0)
- Hi-hat → wherever it is in the overheads, don't try to match mic position
- Make stereo tracks from mono doubles before processing

## SERBAN GHENEA — Modern Pop Mixing Master
Credits: Taylor Swift, The Weeknd, Katy Perry, Bruno Mars, Doja Cat, Troye Sivan

### Core Philosophy
- Fully in-the-box (ITB) — no outboard gear
- Master bus filtering trick: filter low and high end in verses, bypass for chorus — instant impact and size
- Simplicity in arrangement: fewer elements done perfectly beats many elements done adequately
- The three anchors of any mix: kick, bass, lead vocal — everything else supports them
- Vocals forward, wide stereo image, kick/bass locked tight in mono

### Vocal Chain (Serban Method)
- High-pass filter first: 80-150Hz depending on vocalist
- Metric Halo Channel Strip for surgical EQ — narrow Q to notch offensive 800Hz-2.5kHz frequencies
- LA-2A in limit mode (slow attack) → 1176 (fast attack, fast release) in series
  - LA-2A controls and smooths
  - 1176 brings up consonants and detail at word endings
- FabFilter Pro-Q 3 for surgical dynamic EQ work
- De-esser after compression
- Delay: slap around 110ms range
- Reverb: subtle in verse (close/intimate feel), opens up in chorus (sense of space growing)

### Low-End Approach (Serban)
- Kick tuned to key of song — fundamental frequency matches chord root
- Bass notes never clash with kick frequencies — arranged from composition stage
- Kick occupies 0-150Hz in verses, sub bass adds in chorus
- Master bus: high-pass and low-pass filters as AUTOMATION for section contrast

### Stereo Field (Serban)
- Super wide mix — uses stereo width on everything except kick/bass/lead vocal
- Kick and bass drive at constant level throughout track
- Reverb on vocal opens up chorus vs verse for spatial contrast

## BOB CLEARMOUNTAIN — Rock & Pop Pioneer
Credits: Bruce Springsteen, Rolling Stones, David Bowie, Bryan Adams, INXS

### Core Philosophy
- Start with vocals — the lyric and lead vocal matter more than anything
- Think of panning as a stage — place instruments where they'd stand on a live stage
- Listen to the WHOLE song, not individual parts — what pulls your ear away from the vocal?
- "Mix quickly" — get 90% there in the first pass, spend the rest fine-tuning
- Mix songs beginning to end, not in loops — treat it like a live performance

### Workflow
1. Put everything up at once — rough mix to hear what's there
2. Solo individual tracks to understand each track's "role"
3. Set pan positions thinking of it as a stage
4. Get basic drum balance
5. Mix beginning to end without automation first
6. First automation pass: nail the vocal ride
7. Subsequent passes: ride everything else around the vocal

### Stage Placement (Clearmountain)
- Vocals: center (channel 24 on his console)
- Backing vocals: left and right of center
- Guitars: left side
- Drums: further left, percussion far left
- Keyboards: right side
- Strings/horns: far right
- Color code tracks: guitars blue, keys green, drums black

### Philosophy on Parts
- Not every lick needs to be heard — if a guitar part pulls attention from the vocal at an important lyric, pull it back
- Train yourself to hear the song, not individual parts
- Things that are wrong will JUMP OUT when listening to the whole mix
- Make important things louder, unimportant things quieter — that's mixing

## ANDREW SCHEPS — Modern Rock & Pop (Adele, RHCP, Metallica, Jay-Z)

### Parallel Compression (Scheps Method) — His Signature
- Uses ~30 parallel compressors simultaneously as send/return (like reverb sends)
- Direct track stays 100% dry — all compression is blended in from the side
- Never directly compresses tracks (except vocals get direct compression too)
- Shared compressors: kick, bass, drums share some; guitars/synths share others
- The mix "mixes itself" — when vocal is loud it turns guitars down via the shared compressor

### Why Parallel > Insert Compression
- Insert compression catches peaks and turns them DOWN — kills transients
- Parallel compression brings up the QUIET STUFF without touching peaks
- Preserves the uncompressed attack/transient (emotion/feel) while adding density
- You blend HOW MUCH compressed sound you want — infinite control
- "The uncompressed vocal carries all the emotion" — protect it

### Vocal Processing (Scheps)
- Pre-EQ: cut 100Hz shelf (-3dB wide) + boost 8kHz (+3dB)
- Parallel compress aggressively with threshold around -36dB above 1kHz (high-only compression)
- Post-EQ: boost 100Hz (+2.5dB) + gentle high cut at 10kHz (-1.5dB)
- Adds warmth back and removes harshness while keeping presence
- Additional parallel: saturated/distorted version blended under main vocal for warmth

### Drum Setup (Scheps)
- Start with kick and snare LOCKED — everything else built around them
- Overheads and room fill in the space after kick/snare are right
- Parallel distortion on drums (iZotope Trash + heavy EQ before/after) = "room sound without reverb"
- Saturation via Soundtoys Decapitator on drums, guitars, synths for energy and character

### Going ITB (Scheps Lessons)
- UAD 1176 and LA-2A plugins — sometimes prefers to hardware for flexibility/recall
- Waves SSL E-Channel: workhorse channel strip for EQ, compression, gate
- Soundtoys EchoBoy: go-to delay for everything
- FabFilter Pro-Q 3: surgical EQ when needed
- The tools don't matter — the approach does

## MIXED ENGINEER WISDOM

### Universal Truths from the Masters
1. **The vocal is everything** (Clearmountain, Ludwig, CLA) — if the vocal isn't right, nothing else matters
2. **Parallel compression preserves feel** (Scheps) — blend compressed signal, keep dry transients
3. **Less is more** (Rubin, Ghenea) — perfect 8 elements beats mediocre 20
4. **Mix quickly, trust your ears** (Clearmountain, CLA) — first impressions are usually right
5. **Check mono** (Ghenea, everyone) — if it sounds good in mono, it sounds great in stereo
6. **Reference constantly** — A/B against commercial tracks in same genre throughout
7. **Louder is not better** (Ludwig, Scheps) — dynamics are what create impact and emotional response
8. **Automation is mixing** (CLA) — fader rides are MORE important than EQ moves

### Compression Philosophies Compared
- CLA: Aggressive, fast, exciting — 15-20dB GR on vocals, hard parallel on drums
- Scheps: All parallel, preserve transients, blend for density not clamp for control
- Ghenea: Surgical and transparent — LA-2A into 1176 series, controlled and smooth
- Clearmountain: Subtle, musical — compression serves the song not the meter

### The Mix Bus
- CLA: Always mix into SSL comp + limiter + Pultec — threshold -1.5 to -3dB, 2:1
- Scheps: Light overall parallel compression on everything (shared bus)
- Ludwig: Master bus compression should enhance, not define — if you hear it pump, ease off
